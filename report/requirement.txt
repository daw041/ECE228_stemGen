Final Project Grading Rubric and Guidelines Released
Dear Students, 
Below, we provide the grading rubric and guidelines to help you prepare your final projects.

---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Report:
Project Report Rubric (Total: 80 points)
Please submit your final project on both Canvas and Gradescope.
1. Introduction — 8 pts
8 (Excellent): Clearly defines the problem, provides strong and well-motivated background, and convincingly explains why the problem matters. Includes a clear project overview and concise, well-articulated contributions.
6 (Good): Problem and motivation are clear; contributions are present but not sharply articulated.
4 (Fair): Background or motivation is partially clear; contributions are vague or incomplete.
2 (Poor): Problem is unclear; lacks motivation or meaningful overview.
2. Related Work — 4 pts
4 (Excellent): Provides a well-structured synthesis of relevant literature, clearly identifies limitations of prior work, and positions the proposed method effectively.
3 (Good): Covers relevant work but lacks depth or clear comparison.
2 (Fair): Limited or loosely connected discussion of prior work.
1 (Poor): Minimal or incorrect related work.
3. Method / Approach — 32 pts
32 (Excellent): Method is clearly described with strong technical depth and rigor. Formulation, equations, and/or algorithms are precise and well justified. The approach is logically structured and demonstrates strong scientific merit.
24 (Good): Method is mostly clear and technically sound, but lacks depth, clarity, or full justification in some parts.
16 (Fair): Method is partially described but missing key details, structure, or rigor.
8 (Poor): Method is unclear, incomplete, or lacks technical substance.
4. Results & Analysis — 24 pts
24 (Excellent): Results are comprehensive, clearly presented, and well analyzed. Includes sufficient implementation details (dataset, preprocessing, splits, hyperparameters) for reproducibility. Strong quantitative/qualitative evaluation with appropriate baselines and insightful discussion.
18 (Good): Results are clear with reasonable analysis, but lack depth, completeness, or reproducibility details.
12 (Fair): Results are limited, unclear, or weakly analyzed; missing important experimental details.
6 (Poor): Results are insufficient, not reproducible, or not properly analyzed.
5. Conclusion — 4 pts
4 (Excellent): Clearly summarizes findings, reflects on insights and limitations, and proposes meaningful future directions.
3 (Good): Provides a reasonable summary with some reflection.
2 (Fair): Basic summary with limited insight.
1 (Poor): Weak or missing conclusion.
6. Code & GitHub — 8 pts
8 (Excellent): Well-organized repository with clear structure and documentation. README provides clear instructions to run the code and reproduce results. Code is functional and reproducible.
6 (Good): Code is mostly functional with reasonable documentation, but some clarity or usability issues.
4 (Fair): Code is incomplete, poorly documented, or difficult to run.
2 (Poor): Code is missing, non-functional, or not reproducible.
 

---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Presentation:
(1) The format is a highlight presentation. Each group has a maximum of 3 minutes to present their work, followed by a short Q&A on the project content. Given the large number of teams, a strict timing procedure will be enforced. Please practice your presentation to ensure effective communication within the time limit.
(2) Each group will be evaluated by multiple raters, and the final score will be calculated as the average of all ratings.
(3) Make the best use of your three minutes by focusing on the most challenging aspects of your project and the most significant results you achieved. Ensure your presentation has a coherent and logical flow.
